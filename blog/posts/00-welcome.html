Welcome to Ithuvia: the human side of AI in Higher Education

Ithuvia exists for a simple reason: AI is changing learning and teaching faster than most institutions can respond. In that gap, students improvise, faculty experiment in isolation, and leaders draft policy with limited visibility of what is happening on the ground.

This blog will be the most practical part of Ithuvia. Each post will focus on one real problem—assessment design, academic integrity, feedback, workload, or institutional readiness—and offer clear guidance you can implement immediately.

What Ithuvia is (and is not)

  • Ithuvia is a human-centred space for lived academic reality: what works, what fails, and what is ethically defensible.
  • Ithuvia is not an AI hype machine. We will not optimise for novelty—only for usefulness.

Less hype. More how-to.

If you are a student, you should leave with clearer boundaries and better study workflows. If you are faculty, you should leave with better teaching patterns and safer assessment designs. If you are an academic leader, you should leave with sharper institutional questions and evidence of what your policies are producing in practice.
